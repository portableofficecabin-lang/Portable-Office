## Notes and open points

1. **Accounting treatment.** Until dispatch/invoice, these goods stay in **Finished Goods (inventory)** in the seller's books. Revenue is not recognised and no debtor arises, because control has not transferred. Part B is a memorandum record of *sale value*, not an asset at that value.

2. **GST.** No tax invoice has been issued and the goods have not been removed, so the time of supply has not been triggered and **no GST liability has arisen** on these items yet. GST becomes payable when the invoice is raised / the goods are removed, whichever is earlier.

3. **⚠ The ₹15,67,840 total mixes two pricing bases — confirm before invoicing.**
   - The cabin rate of **₹3,20,960 is GST-inclusive**: ₹2,72,000 + 18% GST (₹48,960) = ₹3,20,960 exactly. It matches the eight cabins already invoiced (Sr 235–238, 245–248), so these 4 are the same item at the same all-in rate.
   - **₹94,000** and **₹48,000** do *not* divide cleanly by 1.18, so they look like **base (GST-exclusive)** rates.
   - If that is right, the actual invoice value will be **₹16,18,960**, not ₹15,67,840 — a difference of **₹51,120**:

     | Item | As quoted | If 18% GST added |
     |---|---|---|
